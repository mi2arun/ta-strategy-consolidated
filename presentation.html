First 90 Days
02 / 08

The first 90 days — a phased plan

Sequenced deliberately — a broken process cannot be scaled. First fix the basics, then build the system, then demonstrate results. Early wins build confidence for the larger changes.

🟦 Days 0–30 · Assess & Stabilise

  • Review open roles, time taken at each stage, sources of hire, and attrition by store.
  • Move all hiring onto a single applicant tracking system (ATS).
  • Dedicated team for the 100 new-store roles.
  • Relaunch the employee referral programme with prompt payouts.

🟩 Days 31–60 · Build the System

  • Standardised interview kits + culture-fit scorecards.
  • Maintain pre-screened talent pipelines for high-volume roles.
  • Redesign onboarding: ready from Day 1, with 30/60/90-day check-ins.
  • Introduce AI screening + chatbot; reduce agency dependence.

🟨 Days 61–90 · Demonstrate Results

  • Early wins: time-to-hire ~40 days; fewer offer declines.
  • Launch employer-branding content.
  • Go live with a real-time hiring dashboard for leadership.
  • Present the full 12-month roadmap to leadership.

Faster Hiring
05 / 08

Reducing time-to-hire from 50+ to 30 days

Hiring is slow because we start from scratch for every role and wait too long for manager feedback. The fix is to keep candidates ready and run the stages in parallel, with clear timelines.

⭐ For reference, Chipotle hires in about 4 days while making ~10,000 hires a year — so a 30-day target is well within reach.

🎯 How we accelerate hiring

  • Use pre-screened pipelines for high-volume roles → roles fill in days, not weeks.
  • Run stages in parallel — screening, interview & scheduling together; AI screens 24×7.
  • Set stage timelines — manager feedback in 2 days, offer within 5 days.
  • Instant interview scheduling via chatbot for store roles; a ready panel for leadership.
  • Reduce offer declines 28% → under 12% with a quicker process and clear compensation upfront.
Reducing Attrition
06 / 08

Reducing attrition from 45% to below 25%

Most exits happen in the first three months — about 20% within 45 days, and around 70% of all exits within 90 days. So we must strengthen hiring, onboarding and early support. Retention begins on the day of hire.

🎯 Our three-part approach

  • A · Hire right — assess culture fit, give an honest role preview, and raise referrals to 40%+ (referred hires stay longer).
  • B · Onboard well — ready from Day 1, assign a buddy, and review at 30/60/90 days. One owner for first-90-day retention.
  • C · Retain — a clear growth path (Advisor → Senior → Team Lead → Store Manager) + early warning signals when someone may leave.
  • Prioritise store staff (55%+) and the 11-month tenure issue first.
Technology & Cost
07 / 08

AI drives speed — and reduces cost by half

AI handles the repetitive work — sourcing, screening, scheduling and candidate queries — that currently consumes recruiter time and agency fees. Cost falls mainly through more referrals, fewer agencies, and fewer re-hires.

🤖 Technology stack — phased

  • Phase 1 — full ATS + live dashboards (the data foundation).
  • Phase 2 — AI screening + a WhatsApp/chat assistant for high-volume roles.
  • Phase 3 — AI sourcing + early attrition warning signals.
  • Plus job advertising across social media.

💰 Cost: ₹9,000 → ₹4,500

  • Referrals 20% → 40%+ — the lowest-cost channel, with better retention.
  • Agencies 9–10% → under 4% — bring sourcing & leadership hiring in-house.
  • AI automation — lower cost per candidate at high volume.
  • Lower attrition — each avoided replacement saves a full hiring cost + training time.
🔁 A positive cycle: referrals stay longer → attrition falls → fewer re-hires → recruiters focus on growth rather than replacement → agency budget shifts to lower-cost, higher-quality channels.
