LEAK🪣

The leaky bucket

45% attrition · 55%+ frontline · 11-mo tenure. ~55% of hiring just replaces churn instead of fuelling growth.

SLOW🐢

Reactive & slow

50+ day TAT, weak forecasting, reactive pipelines. Stores open understaffed; panic hiring lowers quality.

COST💸

Expensive & manual

₹9,000/hire · 9–10% agency spend · partial ATS · no AI/analytics · only 20% referral hiring.

💡 Core insight: ~55% of retail hiring is replacement, not growth. Fixing attrition is the single biggest lever to make ~1,000 hires achievable and affordable.

The hiring engine was built for steady-state replacement at ₹3,000 Cr — it processes requisitions reactively, leans on agencies to plug gaps, and has no early-warning data. The jump to ₹5,000 Cr needs a different machine. Critically, the three problems are not independent — they feed a vicious cycle: high attrition forces high volume → volume overwhelms a slow process → slowness forces panic agency hiring at premium cost → rushed, poorly-matched hires churn again. Breaking any one link helps; breaking the attrition link collapses the whole cycle.

🎯 How we solve it

  • Attack attrition first. It's the highest-leverage move — every retained employee is one fewer replacement hire, directly shrinking the ~1,000 number and the cost behind it.
  • Convert ~820 frontline roles to always-on talent pools so demand spikes (new stores) are absorbed from a warm bench, not panic-hired through agencies.
  • Instrument the funnel end-to-end (full ATS + analytics) — TAT by stage, source-of-hire, attrition by store and tenure-band — so we manage by data, not gut feel.
  • Re-channel agency spend into referrals & in-house sourcing, cutting cost and raising quality at the same time.

The 90-day plan is deliberately sequenced — you cannot scale a broken process. We stabilise and instrument first, build the repeatable engine second, and only then push for scale and prove results. Quick, visible wins in the first 30 days (referral relaunch, a war-room on new-store roles) buy the credibility and air-cover needed for the deeper systemic fixes that follow.

🎯 Why this sequence works

  • Days 0–30 — fix visibility & stop the worst bleeding before changing anything structural. You can't aim a fix you can't measure, so data and ATS come first.
  • Days 31–60 — install durable machinery (structured hiring, talent pools, onboarding redesign, AI screening) now that data exists to point it at the right roles.
  • Days 61–90 — demonstrate movement & lock commitment. Show measurable TAT and offer-drop improvement, then secure leadership sign-off and budget for the 12-month plan.
  • Throughout: the bleeding stores and the 100 new-store-launch roles get dedicated focus so business-critical hiring never stalls during the transition.

Cutting TAT from 50+ to ≤30 days

TAT collapses when you stop starting from zero on every role.

Leadership playbook

Kill offer drop

28%→<12% via faster cycles, transparent comp & warm engagement.

First, diagnose where the 50 days actually goes. In a reactive process the funnel stalls in two predictable places: sourcing starting from zero on every role, and waiting on hiring-manager feedback between stages. So the fix is structural, not "work faster" — pre-build the candidates and run the funnel in parallel under hard SLAs. The 28% offer-drop is part of the TAT problem: the longer and colder the process, the more candidates ghost.

🎯 The concrete TAT plan

  • Pipeline, not posting: maintain pre-screened talent pools for the ~820 volume roles so time-to-fill becomes days, not weeks.
  • Parallel funnel: screen, assess and schedule concurrently, with AI doing first-pass screening 24/7 instead of a recruiter working sequentially.
  • SLA-locked stages: hiring-manager feedback within 48 hours, interview-to-offer within 5 days — tracked, with automatic escalation on breach.
  • Instant frontline scheduling via chatbot on day of application; a standing exec-search bench for leadership so senior roles don't drag.
  • Cut offer-drop 28%→<12% with faster cycles, transparent comp up front, and warm candidate engagement right up to Day 1.

Attrition is front-loaded — verified data shows ~20% of new hires leave within 45 days and ~70% of all leavers within 90 days, when day-1 reality fails to match the hiring promise. So the framework attacks all three stages where tenure is won or lost: who we hire, how we onboard, and whether people see a future. Retention is not only HR's job — it begins at the point of hire. We sequence the work to hit the 55%+ frontline and the 11-month tenure cliff first for fastest impact.

🎯 The three-stage framework

  • A · Hire right: culture-fit & realistic-job-preview screening + 40%+ referrals (referred hires stay longer) → far fewer mismatched early-quitters.
  • B · Onboard right: structured Day-1 readiness, a buddy system, and 30/60/90 check-ins — with a named owner accountable for the 90-day stick rate (our key leading indicator).
  • C · Retain right: a published career ladder (Advisor→Senior→Team Lead→Store Manager), manager-capability building, and early-warning attrition analytics to intervene before people quit.
  • Milestones: 45% → ~35% at 12 months → <25% at 24 months, focusing first on the worst-bleeding stores.
08 · Technology, AI & Cost · Q4–Q5

AI drives the speed — and halves the cost

AI for speed & volume, humans for judgment. The same automation that cuts TAT also takes cost/hire from ₹9,000 → ₹4,500.

🤖 The recruitment tech stack

  • Full ATS — single source of truth, automated workflows.
  • AI sourcing + screening — 24/7 first-pass on ~820 volume roles.
  • Conversational chatbot — apply, screen & schedule instantly.
  • Recruitment analytics — funnel + attrition prediction, live dashboards.
  • Employer-brand tools — programmatic ads & social distribution.

💰 Cost: ₹9,000 → ₹4,500

  • Referrals 20% → 40%+ — cheapest channel & highest retention.
  • Consultant 9–10% → <4% — in-house sourcing & leadership hiring.
  • AI automation — lower cost-per-touch at volume.
  • Lower attrition — every avoided replacement saves a full hire + ramp-up.
🔁 The flywheel: referrals stay longer → attrition falls → fewer re-hires → recruiters focus on growth, not churn → agency budget reallocated to cheaper, stickier channels.

The economics are simple: AI absorbs the repetitive, high-volume work — sourcing, screening, scheduling, candidate FAQs — that today consumes recruiter time and agency fees, so the same team handles more hires at lower cost. Cost reduction is mostly a by-product of three shifts (more referrals, fewer agencies, less rework from lower attrition) that compound into a flywheel. We roll out tech in phases so each layer has the data and adoption it needs to work.

🤖 Phased tech rollout

  • Phase 1: full ATS + live dashboards — kill manual reporting, create the data spine.
  • Phase 2: AI screening + conversational chatbot on the volume funnels.
  • Phase 3: AI sourcing + predictive attrition analytics for proactive pipelines.

💰 Cost levers (₹9,000→₹4,500)

  • Referrals 20%→40%+ via an instant-payout scheme — cheapest, stickiest channel.
  • Agency 9–10%→<4% by in-sourcing sourcing & leadership search.
  • Lower attrition — the largest hidden saving: every avoided replacement saves a full cost-of-hire plus ramp-up loss.

Governance is what stops a transformation sliding back. We run a fixed cadence — weekly operational, monthly strategic — with named owners and escalation rules, and the risk register is reviewed in the same forum so mitigations get funded, not just listed. The single most important leading indicator is the 90-day stick rate: it predicts attrition months before it shows up in the annual number, giving us time to intervene.

🎯 The operating rhythm

  • Weekly war-room: open reqs vs target, TAT by stage, offers made/accepted/dropped, pipeline coverage, and new-store readiness.
  • Monthly business review with CEO/CHRO: the strategic KPI table, trend lines vs targets, and risk-register status.
  • Every red metric has an owner and a 2-week corrective action; SLA breaches escalate automatically.
  • Tie ATS adoption & feedback-SLA compliance to manager scorecards so the system actually gets used — the most common reason TA transformations fail.
